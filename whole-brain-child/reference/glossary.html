Glossary

Quick reference for whole-brain parenting terms

Brain Regions

Left Brain
Logical, linguistic, linear. Handles words, sequences, cause-and-effect. "The letter of the law."
Right Brain
Emotional, holistic, nonverbal. Processes feelings, images, gut reactions, bodily sensations. "The spirit of the law."
Upstairs Brain (Prefrontal Cortex)
The thinking part. Decision-making, planning, empathy, self-understanding, moral reasoning. Still under construction in young children.
Downstairs Brain
The survival part. Basic instincts, strong emotions, fight-flight-freeze responses. Operates automatically.
Corpus Callosum
Bundle of fibers connecting left and right brain. The "bridge" that allows integration between hemispheres.

Core Concepts

Integration
Linking different parts of the brain to work together. The goal of whole-brain parenting.
Horizontal Integration
Left brain and right brain working together. Logic + emotions in harmony.
Vertical Integration
Upstairs brain and downstairs brain working together. Thoughtful responses + healthy instincts.
Attunement
Deeply connecting with another person so they "feel felt." Right brain to right brain.
Mindsight
The ability to perceive the mind — your own and others'. Central to the whole-brain approach.
