The Integration Framework

All 12 strategies serve one goal: integration.

Integration means bringing different parts together so they work as a whole. In your toddler's brain, this means:

Left + Right

Logic + Emotion working together

Upstairs + Downstairs

Thinking + Feeling in balance

Implicit + Explicit

Unconscious + Conscious memory

Me + We

Self + Others connected

All 12 Strategies

Chapter 2: Integration #1
Left and Right Brain
  • 1 Connect and Redirect — When upset, connect emotionally first (right brain), then teach (left brain)
  • 2 Name It to Tame It — Tell the story of what happened to help make sense of big emotions
Chapter 3: Integration #2
Upstairs and Downstairs Brain
  • 3 Engage, Don't Enrage — Ask questions, offer choices, engage thinking brain
  • 4 Use It or Lose It — Give daily opportunities to practice decision-making
  • 5 Move It or Lose It — Use movement to regain balance when overwhelmed
Chapter 4: Integration #3
Memory
  • 6 Remote Control — Help replay painful memories with pause, fast-forward, rewind
  • 7 Remember to Remember — Build memory through daily storytelling and questions
Chapter 5: Integration #4
The Many Parts of Self
  • 8 Let the Clouds Roll By — Feelings are temporary, they come and go
  • 9 SIFT — Notice Sensations, Images, Feelings, Thoughts
  • 10 Exercise Mindsight — Return to the calm hub through breathing and presence
Chapter 6: Integration #5
Self and Other
  • 11 Family Fun Factor — Play builds positive relational circuits
  • 12 Connect Through Conflict — See through eyes, read body language, make repair
The Key Insight

The goal isn't perfect parenting. It's good enough parenting with good repair. Every mistake is an opportunity to teach resilience, repair, and growth.

"Even the hard times you go through with your kids, even the mistakes you make as you parent, are opportunities to help your children grow, learn, and develop."

— Siegel & Bryson

What You Now Know

You know how the brain works. You have 12 practical strategies. You understand that every interaction shapes your toddler's developing brain. You are not just surviving — you're building their future.
