Clanker Engineer

A curriculum for the new SDLC. 6 lessons, ~90 minutes, 5 reframes. The bottleneck moved. The job is no longer to type code. The job is to frame problems, direct AI processes, and own outcomes. Most engineering culture is still tuned for the old job. This curriculum is what to do instead.

Lessons

Lesson 0001 clanker 2026-06-29

The Bottleneck Always Moves

The rate-limiting step in software development shifted from executing to framing. This lesson names the new bottleneck and explains why that changes what an engineer's job actually is.

Lesson 0002 clanker 2026-06-29

Learning as the Collateral

The curriculum's claim is that the centaur model left junior engineers with rich collateral learning; the augmented model leaves them with thin collateral unless deliberate training is added.

Lesson 0003 clanker 2026-06-29

Responsibility Can't Be Delegated

The non-negotiable reframe: the human who ships owns the outcome, regardless of who or what produced the artifacts. You cannot delegate your responsibility by handing work to an AI.

Lesson 0004 clanker 2026-06-29

The End of the Centaur

Pair programming's form has to change. The collaborative artifact moved from the keyboard to the spec. The three collaboration shapes: pure human, centaur, augmented.

Lesson 0005 clanker 2026-06-29

Hiring 1,000 Interns

The three-mode interview model and the inverted seniority mix. Farhan's analogy: working with AI is like managing a thousand interns who can each do a task but can't own the outcome.
