The grill-with-docs Skill

Challenge plans against your project's domain model

To invoke:

/grill-with-docs [plan or idea]

The Core Idea Your project has a language. Does the plan speak it? grill-with-docs challenges plans against your project's documented decisions, domain model, and conventions.

What Gets Challenged

CONTEXT.md

Why things are the way they are

ADRs

Architecture decisions

Domain Model

Terms, concepts, relationships

Coding Standards

Conventions and patterns

The Dialogue Pattern

/grill-with-docs Let's add a "like" feature to posts
Let me check your domain model... You have "reactions" defined in CONTEXT.md, not "likes". Why a new term instead of using reactions?
I didn't know reactions existed. What's the difference?
Your ADR-012 says reactions are aggregated counts, not per-user records. Does "like" need to track who liked, or just count?
Your domain model defines User -> Reaction -> Post. Does this new feature fit that relationship, or extend it?

grill-with-docs vs grill-me

grill-with-docs

  • Challenges against existing docs
  • Checks terminology
  • Validates against ADRs
  • Preserves decisions made
  • Checks domain consistency

grill-me

  • Challenges with questions
  • Exposes assumptions
  • Validates feasibility
  • Creates new decisions
  • Checks logical consistency

Why It Matters

  • Consistency — Same terms, same patterns across the codebase
  • Preserves decisions — Don't re-litigate what was already decided
  • Prevents drift — Catch deviations early
  • Knowledge transfer — Learn what others already learned
If grill-with-docs finds a conflict, you have options: update the plan to match docs, update docs (with reason), or document why this is an exception.
Use after grill-me. grill-me challenges the logic, grill-with-docs challenges against your project. Together they cover most bases.

When to Use

  • After brainstorming, before implementation
  • When introducing new concepts or terms
  • When plans might conflict with existing decisions
  • When onboarding to a new codebase
  • Before creating new documentation
