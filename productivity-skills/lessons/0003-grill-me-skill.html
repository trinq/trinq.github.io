The grill-me Skill

Stress-test any plan until it breaks

To invoke:

/grill-me [plan or idea]

The Core Idea No idea survives first contact with reality. Grill-me interrogates your plan relentlessly — exposing flaws, assumptions, and edge cases before you build.

The Questions to Ask

Why?

Why is this the right solution? What problem does it solve? Why now?

What?

What exactly are we building? What are the acceptance criteria? What does success look like?

Who?

Who benefits? Who builds it? Who maintains it? Who does this hurt?

How?

How do we implement it? How long? How much? How do we measure success?

What if?

What if it fails? What if scale 10x? What if the team changes? What if requirements change?

The Dialogue Pattern

/grill-me I'm going to add Redis caching to speed up our API
Why Redis specifically over in-memory caching?
We need it to be distributed across instances.
What happens when Redis goes down? Do you have a fallback? What's your cache invalidation strategy? What keys need invalidation? What about cache stampede?
How will you measure if this works? What's your baseline latency? What p95/p99 are you targeting? How will you know if cache is actually hit?
Who's going to maintain this? What's the oncall story if Redis has issues at 3am? Do they know Redis?

Grilling vs Debating

✓ Grill-Me

  • Questions expose gaps
  • Uncover assumptions
  • Find edge cases
  • Strengthen the plan
  • Shared understanding

✗ Debate

  • Arguments prove superiority
  • Defend a position
  • Win/lose dynamic
  • May reject good ideas
  • No shared understanding

When to Grill

  • Before building anything significant
  • When you feel uncertain about a decision
  • After brainstorming — before implementation
  • When someone says "let's just build it"
  • When requirements feel incomplete
Grill yourself first. Before invoking /grill-me, try to answer these questions yourself. If you can't, that's where to focus.
Grill the best option, not every option. After brainstorming, pick the best idea and grill that one hard.

The Output

After grilling, you should have:

  • Clear requirements — what success looks like
  • Known unknowns — what you still need to figure out
  • Edge cases — what could go wrong
  • Success metrics — how to measure success
  • Shared understanding — everyone aligned
