Finding Your Unknowns

The user-side of agentic coding with Fable 5. Five short lessons (~10–14 min each) on the techniques that determine what the model is actually asked. Iterate, don't plan. Reduce the unknowns in your prompts before, during, and after implementation.

Lessons

Lesson 0001 fable-unknowns 2026-07-04

The Map vs. The Territory

The mental model. The map is what you give the model (prompt, skills, context, history); the territory is where the work happens (the codebase, real constraints). The 2×2 of unknowns. Iterate-don't-plan.

Lesson 0002 fable-unknowns 2026-07-04

Blindspot Pass and Interview

Two pre-implementation techniques for Unknown Unknowns. Blindspot pass runs early as a broad, low-stakes scan; interview runs late as one question at a time. Together they surface things you haven't considered without wasting cycles.

Lesson 0003 fable-unknowns 2026-07-04

Brainstorm, Prototype, Reference

Three techniques for Unknown Knowns. Brainstorm for wildly different directions you can react to. Prototype to isolate the unknown-known surface with fake data. Reference to point at existing code that demonstrates the desired behavior.

Lesson 0004 fable-unknowns 2026-07-04

The Implementation Plan

The most underestimated artifact in agentic coding. Lead with the decisions you'll tweak (data model, type interfaces, user-facing). Bury the mechanical. HTML over markdown, named verification. The plan is a deliverable that travels.

Lesson 0005 fable-unknowns 2026-07-04

Shipping and Closing the Loop

The series finale. Implementation notes (during) capture surprises and deviations. Pitch, explainer, post-quiz (post) ship the change with the next person in mind. Every surprise deserves a log line; every shipped change deserves an artifact.
