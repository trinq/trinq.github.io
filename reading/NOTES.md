# NOTES.md

Working notes about the user, captured during the mission interview on 2026-06-20.

- **Reading volume is not the goal.** The user reads 25+ nonfiction books per year. Don't pitch "speed reading" or "read more" framings — they explicitly don't need to read faster or more.
- **Retention is the goal.** The user reports that books "blur together within weeks." Lessons should be evaluated against whether they reduce the blur, not against reading speed.
- **Tool: NotebookLM is on the table.** The user has an active account. Don't teach a tool-agnostic version of the workflow. The 4-prompt sequence should be taught in NotebookLM-native language.
- **Reading is for personal growth, not professional use.** Optimise examples and assignments for use in the user's own life decisions, not for citation in reports or work artifacts.
- **Time budget: under 1 hour/day.** Lessons should be completable in 15–30 minutes. Tasks that involve reading a real book should add no more than 30–60 minutes on top.
- **Learning style: doing over lecturing.** The user wants the next lesson to be designed around whatever friction they hit running this one, not pre-planned from a syllabus. Honour that — resist over-designing lesson 0002.
