Method 1: Design Skills

Lesson 0002 · ~5 minutes · Beginner

This is the fastest method in the framework. If you're short on time, working on something small, or just need a decent result quickly, design skills are your starting point.

What Are Design Skills?
Prebuilt prompts or workflows that add design intelligence to an AI tool. They encode the taste of their creators.

The Process

1
Add a design skill — Install it in your AI tool (Cursor, Claude, etc.)
2
Give the AI context — What you're building, who it's for, any constraints
3
Prompt a few times — Create your site, app, or poster iteratively
4
Fix obvious issues — Quick cleanup pass for anything that feels wrong
5
Ship it — This method prioritizes speed over perfection

When to Use This Method

Design skills work best when:

  • You're prototyping quickly and need to see something fast
  • The project is small (landing page, simple tool, internal app)
  • You're testing an idea before investing more time
  • Perfect isn't the goal — functional and decent is enough
The Honest Trade-off

The result will usually be decent. It may still feel similar to other AI-generated designs. Good skills help avoid the worst mistakes and "slop," but they don't guarantee distinctiveness.

What You're Borrowing

Remember the taste gap from Lesson 0001? When you use a design skill, you're borrowing taste from its creator. Impeccable has Impeccable's taste. Emil Kowalski's skills have Emil Kowalski's taste.

This is fine for many use cases. But if your project needs to feel uniquely yours, you'll need to inject your own taste — which brings us to the other two methods.

Exercise: Try a Design Skill

Practice · 15 minutes
Build a landing page using one design skill.

Pick a design skill from the list above and install it in Cursor or Claude.

Choose a simple product idea (a fictional SaaS tool, a personal project, a hobby site).

Follow the 5-step process:

  1. Give clear context about what you're building
  2. Prompt 2-3 times to generate the design
  3. Note what feels good and what feels generic
  4. Fix the most obvious issues

Save your output. You'll compare it to work from the other methods later.

Key Takeaways

  • Design skills are the fastest path to a decent result
  • You're borrowing the creator's taste — not generating your own
  • Best for prototyping, small projects, and time-constrained work
  • Limitations become clear when distinctiveness matters
