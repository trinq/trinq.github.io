Method 3: Inspiration Board

Lesson 0003 · ~7 minutes · Intermediate

Design skills are fast but generic. Component-by-Component is high-quality but slow. Inspiration Board sits in the middle — faster than building everything piece by piece, but more distinctive than using a prebuilt skill.

The Core Idea
Instead of telling AI what your design should look like, show it. Curated references encode taste in a way prompts can't.

Why Inspiration Boards Work

When you describe a design in words, you lose information. "Minimal, modern, premium" could mean a hundred different things. But when you show AI a screenshot of a specific website, it can analyze the actual spacing, typography choices, color relationships, and structural patterns.

References don't just communicate style — they communicate taste. A board full of Awwwards winners tells AI something different than a board full of corporate SaaS sites. The selection itself is a taste decision.

Each platform serves a different purpose:

  • Mobbin — Real product screens. Best for app and mobile references.
  • Awwwards — High-end web design. Best for visual sophistication.
  • Webflow Templates — Structure and layout patterns.
  • Craftwork — Components and UI kits.
  • Rebrand Gallery — Branding and identity work.
  • Component Gallery — Atomic design patterns.
  • Savee — General visual inspiration.
  • Cosmos — Curated design systems.
  • Pinterest — Mood boarding and exploration.

The Process

1
Give full context first — What you're building, audience, feeling you want
2
Let the AI ask questions — It can clarify direction before generating
3
Curate 5-10 references — Screenshots that capture the direction you want
4
Prompt for combination — "Combine the style and direction of these references"
5
Refine — Iterate until it feels right

The Key Prompt

After showing your references, use a prompt like:

"Combine the style and direction of these references into a design for my product. Do not copy directly — adapt the patterns to fit my branding and content."

The "do not copy directly" instruction is important. You want AI to extract patterns, not reproduce screens verbatim.

When to Use This Method

Inspiration Board works best when:

  • You have strong references in mind but don't want to build everything manually
  • The project is mid-complexity (full landing page, multi-section site)
  • You want more distinctiveness than a design skill provides
  • You're willing to curate references as part of your workflow

Exercise: Build with an Inspiration Board

Practice · 25 minutes
Curate references and prompt for a combined design.

Use the same product idea from Lesson 0002's exercise.

Step 1: Curate 5-7 screenshots from Mobbin, Awwwards, or Webflow that capture the direction you want.

Step 2: Write context for your AI tool (product, audience, feeling).

Step 3: Share references and prompt: "Combine the style and direction of these references into a design for my product. Do not copy directly."

Step 4: Iterate 2-3 times based on output.

Compare this to your Design Skills output from Lesson 0002. What feels different?

Key Takeaways

  • References encode taste more effectively than prompts
  • Curating the board is itself a taste decision
  • Faster than Component-by-Component, more distinctive than Design Skills
  • "Do not copy directly" is a critical instruction
